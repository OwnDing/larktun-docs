To make the help center valuable quickly, it helps to fill in the following topics first:

{/* truncate */}

1. Product positioning, core concepts, and the first onboarding path.
2. High-priority troubleshooting content, especially for access failures, permission issues, and log-based diagnosis.
3. Showcase stories and best practices that help visitors understand real outcomes and usage boundaries.

If the product has a release rhythm, the blog should also carry release notes and migration guidance so the history stays easy to follow.
