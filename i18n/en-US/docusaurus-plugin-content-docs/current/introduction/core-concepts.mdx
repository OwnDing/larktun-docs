# Core Concepts

To keep docs, blog posts, and showcase content aligned, it helps to define a few key terms up front.

## Project or workspace

Use this to separate teams, business lines, or environments. Entry points, access policies, and logs can all be organized around the project boundary.

## Service entry point

The service entry point is the public-facing address. It usually carries the domain, protocol, authentication method, and forwarding rules.

## Upstream service

The upstream service is the actual workload behind the entry point, such as a local development server, a test API, or an internal admin console.

## Access policy

Access policy defines who can connect, when they can connect, and from where. In a real product guide, this should expand into roles, expiration windows, and audit expectations.

## Diagnostic data

Diagnostic data includes connection status, access logs, error context, and health checks. It becomes the basis for the troubleshooting section.

## Documentation writing guidance

- Explain the business goal before the configuration details.
- Avoid listing parameters without showing a real scenario.
- Add validation steps and failure paths after important actions.
